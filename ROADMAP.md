## Erika's original roadmap

Erika laid out a monthly sequence beginning in November 2025. The descriptions
below preserve the roadmap supplied for this project, with light punctuation,
spacing and grammar cleanup. The same outline is also visible in the public
[description of the mammal lesson](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TuWlGUq5Wi4),
where it is explicitly described as subject to minor changes.

| Original month | Planned lesson | Erika's outline |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **November 2025** | **History of Evolutionary Thought** | This will be a tour through the naturalists and scientists who discovered that species change over time, from the Greeks through Darwin. |
| **December 2025** | **Mechanisms of Evolution—Mutation** | Here we establish what mutations are and how they drive evolutionary change by investigating DNA replication and protein synthesis. Real-world examples of major evolutionary change by small genetic tweaks will be discussed. |
| **January 2026** | **Mechanisms of Evolution—Natural Selection, Gene Flow, Genetic Drift** | The other three major mechanisms of evolution are outlined, including how each of them drives evolutionary change. |
| **February 2026** | **Introduction to Paleontology, Geology, and Biogeography** | A primer on dating methods—how we know the Earth is 4.5 billion years old—how that bears on fossils and fossil formation, and how living and extinct species in physical space bolster evolutionary theory. Include case studies on the fish–tetrapod transition and whale evolution. |
| **March 2026** | **Nested Hierarchies—Genes and Traits** | A primer on taxonomy and phylogeny using genes and physical characteristics. How do we get “species-level” family trees? Why can we trust they are accurate? |
| **April 2026** | **Ontogeny and Evolutionary Legacies** | A primer on how development—genetic pathways and physical characteristics—bolsters evolutionary theory. Case studies on how our bodies, and those of other animals, belie our evolutionary past. |
| **May 2026** | **A Tour through Deep Time** | A brief tour through deep time with a handful of small case studies on reconstructing ancient environments, mass extinctions and major fossil transitions of importance. Stop at the base of our major case study in evolution: humans. |
| **June 2026** | **The Continuum of Human Behavior** | How unique are humans with regard to other life? Is consciousness found elsewhere? Theory of mind? Empathy and cruelty? Creativity and religion? Language? |
| **July 2026** | **Human Evolution: Apes and First Hominins** | Where and when did humans split from other apes? What were the first members of our lineage like? Pulls from all previous lessons. |
| **August 2026** | **Human Evolution: Australopithecus and Kin** | When did our relatives begin walking upright and using tools? How do we know these apes are related to us? Pulls from all previous lessons. |
| **September 2026** | **Human Evolution: Genus Homo and Us** | When did the first “humans” emerge and why? Why are we the only ones left? How does evolution explain modern human diversity? Pulls from all previous lessons. |
| **October 2026** | **Putting It All Together** | A synthesis of the previous eleven lessons as a capstone. |
| **November 2026** | **Post-course discussion** *(editorial label)* | Will and I will discuss where we still disagree! |

The October capstone completes the original twelve-lesson teaching arc. The
following November discussion is a post-course comparison, not a thirteenth
content lesson in that outline.

## How the published course has changed

The first three recordings followed the planned monthly subjects closely. From
February onward, topics expanded into longer standalone case studies instead of
remaining inside the original single-month survey slots.

| Calendar slot | Original plan | What was actually published |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nov 2025 | History of evolutionary thought | Lesson 1: history of thought. |
| Dec 2025 | Mutation | Lesson 2: mutations, inheritance and molecular genetics. |
| Jan 2026 | Selection, gene flow and drift | Lesson 3: natural selection and the other population mechanisms. |
| Feb 2026 | Paleontology, geology, biogeography, dating, tetrapods and whales | Lesson 4 concentrated on the age of the Earth, geological reasoning and dating evidence. |
| Mar 2026 | Nested hierarchies | Lesson 5 became a complete whale-evolution case study. |
| Apr 2026 | Ontogeny and evolutionary legacies | Lesson 6 became a complete bird-evolution case study, including developmental evidence. |
| May 2026 | Tour through deep time | No course livestream was published in May. |
| Jun 2026 | Continuum of human behaviour | Lesson 7 became a complete mammal-evolution case study. |
| Jul 2026 | Apes and first hominins | Lesson 8 covered the fish–tetrapod transition and experimental multicellularity. |

This is schedule drift, not evidence that the omitted headings were abandoned.
Erika explains that the case-study middle of the course grew because of the
density of the material ([lesson 8, 2:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJofeBRFwvI&t=165s)).
She had also warned that time, illness and travel might require her to condense,
extend or cut later material ([lesson 7, 1:55:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TuWlGUq5Wi4&t=6928s)).
Until Erika publishes a revised sequence, the original outline records intended
coverage but no longer predicts one topic per original calendar month.

### Why there is no day-of-month prediction

The recorded dates—3 November, 3 December, 7 January, 4 February, 11 March,
22 April, 1 June and 8 July—show a broadly monthly intention but not a stable
day, weekday or interval. Guessing a specific August date would therefore add
false precision. This file will replace the month window with an exact date only
when Erika or the channel publishes one.

## Updating this roadmap

When a new lesson is announced or recorded:

1. verify the statement on Erika's channel or in the latest lesson;
2. distinguish an exact announcement from a cadence estimate;
3. move a published stream into the recorded table with its exact title, video
   ID, public date and runtime;
4. link its completed lesson folder;
5. revise the plan-versus-publication table without rewriting the historical
   original roadmap; and
6. update the checked date, total runtime, root README, livestream catalogue and
   course map together.

Editors adding the next revision package should follow the complete workflow and
quality gates in [AGENTS.md](AGENTS.md).
