# Evolution revision guide

Detailed, source-linked revision notes for Gutsick Gibbon's livestream course
teaching evolutionary theory and the age of the Earth to Will Duffy of
The Final Experiment.

The guide turns long-form lessons into manageable study sections without
detaching Erika's explanations from their source. Important claims, examples,
qualifications, and Will–Erika exchanges have click-to-play timestamps. Images
are stored locally and credited where they appear; diagrams are used to clarify
processes, branching relationships, and converging evidence.

> **Current coverage — 15 July 2026:** eight recorded lessons, totalling
> **37:14:14**. See the [course roadmap](ROADMAP.md) for recording dates, direct
> livestream links, the remaining lesson plan, and the confidence attached to
> any future date estimate.

## What is inside a lesson folder

A lesson folder is a complete revision package for one livestream, not a brief
summary. Its README introduces the lesson and gives a recommended route through
the relevant topic notes; the later lesson packages also include clock-range
maps for direct navigation.

| File | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `README.md` | Lesson overview and study route; where available, runtime, scope, audience-Q&A cutoff, and a clock-range stream map. |
| `00-wills-opening.md` | A careful account of Will's presentation before Erika begins teaching, including the claims and questions that frame the lesson. |
| `01-*.md`, `02-*.md`, … | Detailed concept notes in teaching order, with explanations, precise timestamp links, inline sources, real images, diagrams, and recall prompts. |
| `will-duffy-qa.md` | Will's questions and Erika's answers from the teaching exchange. The later audience and superchat Q&A is excluded. |
| `images/` | Downloaded stream frames or externally sourced visuals used inline in that lesson. Every embed has source and rights information beside it. |

The files are split by coherent ideas rather than by arbitrary time blocks. A
topic note is intentionally detailed enough to revise on its own, while the
nearby timestamps make it easy to hear Erika's complete wording when nuance or
context matters.

## How the guide handles evidence and attribution

### Livestream-grounded explanations

The videos are the guide's principal sources. Notes paraphrase rather than
pretend to be verbatim transcripts. A timestamp attached to a paragraph means
that the linked portion of the stream supports the attributed explanation; it
does not imply that every sentence is a direct quotation.

English YouTube auto-captions were used as a searchable index and checked
against the stream when specialist names, numbers, citations, or disputed
wording mattered. Auto-captions can change and can misrecognise technical terms,
so they are not committed as part of this repository. The
[timestamped transcript index](sources/timestamped-transcript-index.md) explains
how to retrieve a current copy for verification.

### Erika, Will, and the guide's own synthesis

The notes distinguish among:

- Will's opening position or question;
- Erika's explanation or reply;
- a revision summary connecting several parts of the course; and
- independently sourced scientific context or further reading.

This separation matters. A scientifically accurate detail should not be
attributed to Erika unless it appears in the recording. Conversely, Will's
questions are preserved as questions rather than flattened into claims he did
not make.

### External sources

Where Erika or Will identifies a paper, researcher, museum specimen, diagram,
or named case, the relevant lesson links it at the point of use whenever it can
be identified reliably. Primary papers, museums, universities, geological
surveys, and other authoritative sources are preferred. If an auto-caption does
not preserve enough detail to identify a reference with confidence, the note
states what is known and labels possible matches or further reading instead of
inventing certainty.

### Visuals

No AI-generated imagery is used. Images are downloaded into the lesson that
uses them so the guide does not rely on fragile remote embeds. Each appearance
includes meaningful alt text and a nearby credit with the original source,
licence or public-domain status, and any relevant resizing or conversion note.
Mermaid diagrams are original explanatory schematics; they do not replace the
underlying observation or source.

## Repository map

```text
README.md                 this entry point
ROADMAP.md                recording history and cautiously estimated next steps
GLOSSARY.md               comprehensive cross-course terminology reference
APPENDIX.md               technical tables, notation, methods, and quick reference
AGENTS.md                 project contract and future-lesson workflow
lessons/                  full, source-facing notes for each livestream
  <nn-topic>/images/      local, credited visuals embedded by that lesson
docs/                     cross-course explanations and study routes
  case-studies/           lineage-focused comparisons
  revision/               checklist and glossary
sources/                  video catalogue, transcript index, and reading guide
LICENSE                   repository licence
```

The layers have different jobs:

- **`lessons/`** preserves the depth, order, examples, and discussion of each
  livestream.
- **`docs/`** synthesises ideas across streams for revision.
- **`sources/`** makes provenance, discovery, and independent checking easier.
- **`ROADMAP.md`** tracks what exists and separates public plans from estimates.
- **`GLOSSARY.md`** supplies the complete terminology reference; the shorter
  glossary under `docs/revision/` remains an active-recall aid.
- **`APPENDIX.md`** holds reusable technical reference material that would
  otherwise interrupt the flow of a lesson.

## Scope and limitations

- The course is ongoing. This edition covers the eight streams publicly located
  by the date at the top of this README; it does not imply that the remaining
  planned lessons have been cancelled or scheduled.
- The guide covers Erika's teaching and her discussion with Will. It deliberately
  omits the audience/superchat Q&A at the end of each livestream.
- This is a revision guide to the course, not a substitute for the recordings,
  a complete biology curriculum, or a peer-reviewed systematic review.
- Links and public schedules can change. The roadmap records its verification
  date and the basis for each future-status statement.

## Maintaining or extending the guide

Before changing the project or adding a newly recorded lesson, read
[AGENTS.md](AGENTS.md). It defines the source hierarchy, lesson file contract,
image and citation policy, timestamp format, reference-file maintenance,
future-lesson workflow, and quality gates. New work should deepen the existing
structure rather than add parallel “expanded” notes or detached source maps.
