## Scientific reasoning

| Term | Revision definition | Lesson context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Actualism** | Interpreting ancient traces using physical laws and processes observed today, while allowing rates to vary and including genuine catastrophes. It is not “everything was always slow.” | [Lesson 4, 54:08](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=3248s) |
| **Consilience** | Agreement among partly independent evidence routes with different assumptions and failure modes. | [Reading evidence](../06-reading-the-evidence.md) |
| **Evidence** | An observation or measurement that changes how well a hypothesis is supported relative to alternatives. Evidence is not synonymous with permanent proof. | [Lesson 1, 36:32–37:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=2192s) |
| **Falsifiable** | Exposed to at least one possible observation that would count against the claim. It does not mean “already false.” | [Lesson 1, 31:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=1884s) |
| **Hypothesis** | A provisional, testable proposed explanation for a specified phenomenon. | [Lesson 1, 34:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=2074s) |
| **Law** | A description of a regular relationship, often mathematical. A theory does not graduate into a law. | [Lesson 1, 38:04–38:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=2284s) |
| **Model** | A simplified explanatory or quantitative representation used to generate predictions. Its assumptions and useful range must be stated. | [History and reasoning](../02-history-and-reasoning.md) |
| **Prediction** | An expected observation derived from a model before, or independently of, checking the decisive evidence. A good prediction could have failed. | [Lesson 1, 40:00–41:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=2400s) |
| **Proxy** | A measurable feature used to infer something that is not directly preserved, such as isotopes for temperature or skull cavities for brain shape. | [Mammal case](../case-studies/mammals.md) |
| **Theory** | A broad explanatory framework that integrates many observations and tested hypotheses. In science it does not mean an unsupported hunch. | [Lesson 1, 38:01–38:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=2281s) |

## Genetics and development

| Term | Revision definition | Lesson context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Allele** | A sequence variant at a genetic locus. | [Lesson 2, 45:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=2725s) |
| **Chromosome** | DNA packaged with proteins into a physical inheritance unit. Homologous chromosomes carry corresponding loci. | [Lesson 2, 55:20–58:13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=3320s) |
| **Coding sequence** | DNA whose transcribed information specifies an amino-acid sequence. Coding is not synonymous with all functional DNA. | [Lesson 2, 1:03:54–1:05:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=3834s) |
| **Codon** | A three-base messenger-RNA unit read during translation; several codons can specify the same amino acid. | [Lesson 2, 1:31:47–1:42:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=5507s) |
| **DNA** | Deoxyribonucleic acid, the complementary nucleotide polymer that stores most inherited sequence information in organisms. | [Lesson 2, 1:01:26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=3686s) |
| **Dominant** | Expressed in a heterozygote under the specified inheritance model. It does not mean adaptive, stronger or common. | [Lesson 2, 46:37–47:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=2797s) |
| **Gene** | A DNA region that contributes to a functional product; its expression can depend on regulatory sequence and cell context. A gene is not simply “one visible trait.” | [Lesson 2 notes](../../lessons/02-mutations/02-dna-and-mutation.md) |
| **Gene duplication** | Copying a gene or larger region. Copies may retain, divide, lose or acquire functions. | [Lesson 2, 2:08:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=7681s) |
| **Genome** | The complete DNA complement of an organism or cell, not merely its protein-coding genes. | [Lesson 2, 1:03:02](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=3782s) |
| **Genotype** | The allele combination carried at one or more loci. | [Lesson 2, 45:25–45:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=2725s) |
| **Germ line** | Cells that produce eggs or sperm and can transmit sequence changes to descendants. A body-cell mutation is normally not inherited. | [Lesson 8 review, 1:15:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJofeBRFwvI&t=4501s) |
| **Meiosis** | Cell division that reduces chromosome number to make haploid gametes and includes recombination between homologues. | [Lesson 2, 1:13:49–1:17:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=4429s) |
| **Mutation** | A lasting change in DNA sequence or chromosome structure. Its fitness effect depends on genetic and environmental context. | [Lesson 2 notes](../../lessons/02-mutations/02-dna-and-mutation.md) |
| **Phenotype** | A measured characteristic produced by genotype, development and environment. | [Lesson 2, 45:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=2740s) |
| **Placode** | A local thickening of embryonic skin that begins development of structures such as hair, scales or feathers under different signalling regimes. | [Bird case](../case-studies/birds.md) |
| **Pleiotropy** | One gene or variant affecting more than one phenotypic outcome. | [Lesson 2 inheritance notes](../../lessons/02-mutations/01-inheritance-and-variation.md) |
| **Pseudogene** | A sequence related to a functioning gene but disabled or no longer producing the ancestral functional product. Its inherited changes can retain historical information. | [Whale case](../case-studies/whales.md) |
| **Recombination** | Exchange between homologous chromosomes during meiosis, producing new combinations of existing alleles rather than new sequence letters. | [Lesson 2, 1:16:52–1:17:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=4612s) |
| **Regulatory sequence** | DNA that influences when, where or how strongly a gene is expressed. | [Lesson 2, 1:05:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0&t=3900s) |
| **Transcription** | Synthesis of RNA from a DNA template. | [Lesson 2 DNA notes](../../lessons/02-mutations/02-dna-and-mutation.md) |
| **Translation** | Ribosomal reading of messenger-RNA codons to assemble a peptide. | [Lesson 2 DNA notes](../../lessons/02-mutations/02-dna-and-mutation.md) |

## Population change

| Term | Revision definition | Lesson context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Adaptation** | A heritable feature shaped by selection because it increased reproductive success in a specified historical environment. The same feature may carry costs elsewhere. | [Variation and selection](../03-variation-and-selection.md) |
| **Allele frequency** | The proportion of gene copies in a population represented by a particular allele. Changing frequency is a direct measure of evolution. | [Lesson 3, 33:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=2000s) |
| **Bottleneck** | A sharp population reduction that leaves a chance-biased subset of the earlier genetic diversity. | [Lesson 3 mechanisms](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/01-mechanisms.md) |
| **Fitness** | Relative contribution of descendants to later generations in a specified environment; not strength, virtue or survival alone. | [Lesson 3, 38:20–39:01](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=2300s) |
| **Founder effect** | Drift caused when a small sample establishes a population with allele frequencies unlike the source. | [Lesson 3, 2:24:00–2:25:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=8640s) |
| **Gene flow** | Movement of alleles among populations through migrants or gametes, often reducing divergence. | [Lesson 3 mechanisms](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/01-mechanisms.md) |
| **Gene pool** | The collection and frequencies of alleles present in a population. | [Lesson 3, 33:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=2000s) |
| **Genetic drift** | Chance change in allele representation caused by finite sampling, especially influential in small populations. | [Lesson 3, 2:22:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=8560s) |
| **Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium** | A null expectation for allele and genotype frequencies under idealised absence of selection, mutation, migration, drift and non-random mating. | [Lesson 3 mechanisms](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/01-mechanisms.md) |
| **Natural selection** | Non-random change caused when heritable variants systematically affect reproductive contribution in current conditions. | [Lesson 3 selection](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/02-selection-in-populations.md) |
| **Population** | A connected group through which inherited variants can pass across generations. | [What evolution means](../01-what-evolution-means.md) |
| **Sexual selection** | Frequency change driven by differences in obtaining mates or fertilisations, including competition and mate choice. | [Lesson 8 review, 1:28:42](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJofeBRFwvI&t=5322s) |
| **Stabilising selection** | Selection favouring an intermediate range and removing extremes under relatively stable conditions. | [Lesson 3 selection notes](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/02-selection-in-populations.md) |

## Lineages, trees and speciation

| Term | Revision definition | Lesson context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Anagenesis** | Change along one lineage without a branching event. | [Lesson 3, 3:00:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=10820s) |
| **Clade** | An ancestral population and all of its descendant branches. | [What evolution means](../01-what-evolution-means.md) |
| **Cladogenesis** | Branching in which one lineage gives rise to independently evolving descendant lineages. | [Lesson 3, 3:00:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=10820s) |
| **Common ancestry** | The relationship in which distinct lineages descend from an earlier shared population. | [Lesson 1, 2:15:20](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ&t=8120s) |
| **Crown group** | The last common ancestor of all living members of a named group and all of that ancestor's descendants. | [Mammal case](../case-studies/mammals.md) |
| **Gradualism** | Accumulation of change through smaller generational steps. It does not require one constant rate. | [Lesson 3, 3:01:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=10860s) |
| **Homology** | Correspondence inherited from a shared ancestral structure, tested through position, development, fossils and tree distribution. | [Reading evidence](../06-reading-the-evidence.md) |
| **Analogy** | Similar function or appearance reached through different historical routes, such as powered flight in bats and birds. | [Reading evidence](../06-reading-the-evidence.md) |
| **Phylogeny** | A testable hypothesis about lineage relationships and branch order. | [Reading evidence](../06-reading-the-evidence.md) |
| **Punctuated equilibrium** | A tempo of longer relative stasis interrupted by geologically rapid change, often around isolation or environmental disruption. “Rapid” can still span many generations. | [Lesson 3, 3:01:40–3:02:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=10900s) |
| **Speciation** | The process by which populations become independently evolving and increasingly reproductively isolated lineages. | [Lesson 3 divergence notes](../../lessons/03-natural-selection/03-divergence-and-speciation.md) |
| **Stem group** | Extinct branches closer to a crown group than to another living group but outside the crown itself. “Stem” does not mean inferior. | [Mammal case](../case-studies/mammals.md) |
| **Transitional fossil** | A dated organism with the predicted mosaic of older and newer traits relative to specified groups; it need not be a direct ancestor. | [Lesson 5, 1:09:48–1:16:24](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnY58Y8FJBQ&t=4188s) |
| **Vicariance** | Geographic division of a population by a new barrier, reducing gene flow and permitting divergence. | [Lesson 3, 2:56:36](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2JCO6eXans&t=10596s) |

## Geology and deep time

| Term | Revision definition | Lesson context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Absolute/numerical dating** | Assigning an age or age range in years to a specified event using a calibrated clock. “Absolute” does not mean zero uncertainty. | [Deep time](../05-deep-time-and-converging-evidence.md) |
| **Daughter product** | The nuclide produced by radioactive transformation of a parent isotope. | [Lesson 4, 2:10:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=7832s) |
| **Half-life** | The time over which half the parent atoms in a large radioactive population decay. | [Lesson 4, 2:10:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=7832s) |
| **Index fossil** | A recognisable, widespread fossil restricted to a useful interval and used to correlate separated strata. | [Lesson 4, 2:07:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=7655s) |
| **Parent isotope** | The unstable starting nuclide in a radioactive decay system. | [Lesson 4 dating notes](../../lessons/04-age-of-earth/02-dating-and-cross-checks.md) |
| **Radiometric dating** | Measuring an appropriate parent–daughter isotope system in a mineral or organic sample to estimate time since a defined event. | [Lesson 4, 2:09:31–2:23:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=7771s) |
| **Relative dating** | Ordering events as earlier or later through superposition, cross-cutting, fossils and other relationships without first assigning years. | [Lesson 4 strata notes](../../lessons/04-age-of-earth/01-strata-and-fossils.md) |
| **Stratigraphy** | Study of rock layers, their relationships, environments and correlation through space and time. | [Deep time](../05-deep-time-and-converging-evidence.md) |
| **Superposition** | In an undisturbed sedimentary sequence, lower beds were deposited before upper beds. | [Lesson 4, 1:26:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=5166s) |
| **Unconformity** | A surface representing erosion or non-deposition between older and younger rock units. | [Deep time](../05-deep-time-and-converging-evidence.md) |
| **Varve** | A repeated seasonal sediment couplet, commonly a summer and winter layer, that can provide a counted chronology when its seasonal origin is tested. | [Lesson 4, 1:15:11](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTVFcr4GCMk&t=4511s) |

## Often-confused pairs

| Do not collapse | Distinction |
| --- | --- |
| Evolution / natural selection | Evolution is the population change; selection is one cause of it. |
| Mutation / recombination | Mutation changes sequence; recombination reshuffles existing alleles. |
| Fitness / survival | Fitness concerns descendants; survival can contribute but is not sufficient. |
| Homology / analogy | Homology is inherited correspondence; analogy is similar function reached separately. |
| Transitional / direct ancestor | A transitional mosaic can lie on a close side branch. |
| Relative / numerical age | Relative dating orders events; numerical dating estimates years. |
| Non-coding / non-functional | Some non-coding sequence regulates genes; the terms are not synonyms. |
| Middle ear / inner ear | Malleus, incus and stapes are middle-ear ossicles; the cochlea and vestibular system lie in the inner ear. |
| Crown / stem | Both describe tree position relative to living branches, not progress or quality. |
| Everyday “fish” / Sarcopterygii | The everyday category is ecological and incomplete as a clade; Sarcopterygii includes tetrapods. |
