# Lesson 1 — History of evolutionary thought

[Watch the complete lesson](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XoE8jajLdRQ) (3:31:01).

This lesson builds the reasoning framework used throughout the course. Erika first distinguishes a scientific question from a philosophical or theological one, then follows the ideas that made evolutionary theory possible: change through deep time, fossil extinction, nested classification, common descent and natural selection.

## Study route

1. [Will's opening presentation](00-wills-opening.md) — why he entered the project and what he wanted evidence to establish.
2. [Explaining life scientifically](01-explaining-life.md) — hypotheses, theories, historical inference and the questions evolution addresses.
3. [Classification, species and ancestry](02-classification-and-ancestry.md) — essentialism, fossils, taxonomy and the branching view of life.
4. [From natural theology to Darwin and Wallace](03-darwin-and-natural-selection.md) — deep time, actualism, the voyage of the *Beagle* and Darwin's mechanism.
5. [Will Duffy Q&A](will-duffy-qa.md) — Will's substantive questions and Erika's answers during the teaching portion.

The end-of-stream superchat discussion is outside the revision material. Timestamp links are placed beside the claim or example they support in each note, so a reader can move directly from a revision point to the relevant passage in the lesson.
