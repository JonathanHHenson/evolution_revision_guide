# Lesson 2 — Inheritance and mutation

[Watch the complete lesson](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uQWss3w8x0) (4:32:29).

This lesson connects Darwin's unknown mechanism of heredity to modern genetics. Erika works from Mendel's inheritance experiments through chromosomes, DNA replication and protein synthesis, then shows how point mutations, duplication and regulatory changes produce variation whose effects depend on context.

## Study route

1. [Will's opening presentation](00-wills-opening.md) — his revised design criterion and questions carried forward from lesson 1.
2. [Inheritance and variation](01-inheritance-and-variation.md) — Mendel, cells, meiosis, recombination and genetic family trees.
3. [DNA, genes and mutation](02-dna-and-mutation.md) — DNA structure, protein synthesis, mutation types and gene duplication.
4. [Mutation effects and case studies](03-effects-and-examples.md) — harmful, neutral and beneficial outcomes from single bases to developmental systems.
5. [Will Duffy Q&A](will-duffy-qa.md) — the substantive exchanges during the lesson, excluding superchats.
